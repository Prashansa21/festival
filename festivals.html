Celebrating Festivals: Kali Puja, Diwali, and Durga Puja

Kali Puja

Kali Puja is a festival dedicated to the worship of the goddess Kali. It is celebrated primarily in West Bengal and other parts of India, usually on the new moon night in the month of Kartik.

Key Highlights:

  • Worship of Goddess Kali
  • Rituals performed at night
  • Offerings of flowers, fruits, and sweets

People decorate their homes with lights and flowers, creating a vibrant atmosphere.

Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most popular Hindu festivals. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil, celebrated with fireworks, sweets, and the lighting of lamps.

Key Highlights:

  • Lighting of diyas (lamps)
  • Fireworks and celebrations
  • Exchanging gifts and sweets

Families come together to celebrate, and homes are adorned with colorful rangoli designs.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is an annual Hindu festival originating in the Indian subcontinent. It celebrates the goddess Durga's victory over the buffalo demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

Key Highlights:

  • Elaborate decorations and pandals
  • Cultural performances and music
  • Community feasting and celebrations

The festival lasts for several days, culminating in the immersion of the idol in water, symbolizing the goddess's return to her celestial abode.
